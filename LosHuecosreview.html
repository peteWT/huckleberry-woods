40 Acre Homestead for rent or lease:

After 43 years building a dream homestead in Humboldt County, California near Miranda, an aging couple of back-to-the-landers are offering their home and homestead for long or short term rent or lease. The property includes:

  • A three + bedroom, one bath, 1800 sq ft two story house. It sits on a 40 acre parcel with 38 acres of mixed hardwood, conifer and huckleberry forest and  a lovely long view to the east from the front porch. There is a large,  currently unused, veggie, herb and flower garden,  with a small mixed fruit (apple and cherry trees) orchard and established grape vines. These are complemented by two glass greenhouses. State and county regulations allow cannabis cultivation only for personal use. No commercial cannabis is allowed.
  • Homestead is 2 and a half hours from the San Francisco Bay Area via US 101. It is at the end of a one mile graded and gravel driveway linked to a 5 mile dirt road and then 8 miles of paved road, for a 35 minute drive to US 101, the town of Miranda and the Avenue of the Giant’s Scenic Redwood Drive. The County maintains the paved road and a private road association maintains the trunk dirt road while the one mile driveway is the owner’s responsibility with seasonal maintenance by the tenant.
  • Three gravity fed springs supply fresh pure drinking water. The extensive water system includes two small 25,000 gal agricultural ponds, eight @ 3000 gal above ground tanks and separate 4000 gal of underground drinking water storage tanks. These water systems provide fresh, filtered water to the house, fire water to the three fire hoses and irrigation water to the agriculture.              
  • The bathroom includes a shower/tub combo with double vanity sinks flow to gray water fields.  
  • A composting toilet located on the covered back porch requires minor attention. There is no flush toilet. Annual removal of the humanure will be instructed by the owner.
  • There is a two person outdoor tub for night stargazing.
  • Two propane water heaters backup summertime solar hot water and provide wintertime hot water.
  • A complete off-grid power system includes: 10 solar panels, a 2000 watt inverter and charge controllers, battery backup, a 4000 Watt propane generator and a micro-hydro system in the winter producing 240 Watts day and night.
  • The kitchen is equipped with an electric refrigerator/freezer, a 4 burner gas stove and oven, a double sink and direct-to-the- garden compost disposal. Basic kitchen utensils, dishes, pots and pans would be available if desired.
  • A clothes washer and propane dryer on the covered back porch are included.
  • The house has access to medium speed Internet and good cell reception , but no cable. Super high speed Starlink satellite connection is also purchasable.
  • In the winter, the house uses wood heat: a Monarch Wood Cook Stove and Vermont Castings Vigilant Wood Stove. One stove is adequate most of the winter. Two bedrooms are heated by propane. 3-5 cords of wood are needed for winter wood heating. Abundant hardwood and softwood trees are available for cutting and splitting as the firewood supply. One month’s firewood is stacked nearby in the wood shed.
  • Adjacent to the enclosed generator shed is a 700 sq foot woodshop/ carport.  The shop is equipped with multiple stationary and portable tools. The shop could  be used by the current owner as well as guests/tenents.
  • Currently the house is fully furnished with dining room table and chairs, lunch counter,  living room sofa and chairs, and beds, mattresses (2 queen, one full, one single) in the bedrooms.  The house has been family built using wood, timbers and siding milled from the land. Custom counters, cabinets and window and door trim are all from local hardwood and softwood trees milled on site.
  • Owners intend to remain active in forest management and will retain a small work and stay place separate from the main house and shop.
  • The larger picture:
  • A small PreK-1 grade school and community center is 5 minutes away at the beginning of the homestead’s  driveway. This school has been in existence for several decades and has many wonderful resources and the capacity to expand if the number of young families increases  in the community. The Volunteer Fire Department firehouse is ten minutes away, staffed by an active 10-person all volunteer fire crew. The area is now a registered Fire Wise Community with its own Fire Protection District.There is a weekly Community Exchange/ farmers market in the summer,  an annual fundraiser Halloween Boogie, and various other community events.
  • The town of Miranda has the basic services of a post office, a market, a junior and senior high school, two restaurants, a  drive-in coffee spot, a weekly farmers market, a welding shop and a gas station with three electric car chargers. The larger community of Redway/Garberville is  another 15 minute drive and includes an elementary school, a county library, a natural foods store, a bank and a credit union, shops, and a vibrant arts, dance, and theater ethic, including the Mateel Community Center which hosts well known performing artists. There is a large community park with walking, hiking and biking nature trails and picnic areas.

  • There is great early summer swimming in the Eel River, and many redwood groves to explore.

Shelter Cove, with its beaches, fishing, surfing and restaurants is an hour and a half drive.

The ideal long term renter would be a family with children, possessing woodworking and/or trade skills,  a remote working profession with a stable income and ambitions to eagerly commit to a long-term garden, homestead and country life.  In exchange for no highway or urban noises, the lifestyle requires attention to some maintenance.

Rental or lease price ( $1500/mo.) The extent of the renters’ participation in homestead maintenance chores could reduce the rent. There could also be work/trade around the homestead  considered for a portion of the rent. First month rent, last month rent and damage deposit required. Off grid electricity provided and included in rent.  

Short term rental of the homestead can be considered as a remote refuge and urban getaway. The house is fully equipped except for toothbrushes. One month: $3,000. Two months: $5500. Three months: $8000.
